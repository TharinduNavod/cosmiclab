Cosmic Timeline

13.8 Billion Years Ago - Big Bang

The universe begins from a singularity, expanding and cooling rapidly. All space, time, matter, and energy originate from this moment.

380,000 Years - Recombination

Universe cools enough for atoms to form. Light can finally travel freely, creating the cosmic microwave background we observe today.

200 Million Years - First Stars

The first generation of stars ignite, illuminating the universe and creating heavier elements through nuclear fusion.

4.6 Billion Years Ago - Solar System Forms

Our solar system coalesces from a cloud of gas and dust. Earth forms and life begins within a billion years.

Present Day

Humans explore the cosmos, uncovering the universe's secrets from the quantum to the cosmic scale.

Far Future - Heat Death

The universe continues expanding. Stars will eventually burn out, leading to a cold, dark future trillions of years from now.
